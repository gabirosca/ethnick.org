Global Exposure Program

ETHNICK’s core mission is to increase mutual understanding and sustainable development by using alternative education models, raising awareness and generating direct exposure opportunities for our youth and young professionals.

We look to bring our contribution in the achievement of these goals by developing and offering educational and cultural exchange programs, including the exchange of scholars, researchers, professionals, students and educators.

ETHNICK seeks to establish unique international exposure opportunities and partnerships to benefit a population that is not being addressed through existing authorized exchanges.

The desired result of this program is to develop cultural awareness, improve students’ knowledge base through coursework, as well as direct exposure opportunities and to create a communication platform for students and partner organizations to use in the sharing of valuable information, support and networking.

Students will be tasked with keeping a journal during the trip. The journal will serve two purposes: it will allow students to keep a detailed record of the trip and the information documented in the journal will be the basis for writing a publishable article about their experience. During the trip, students will be given an aspect of culture, government and/or history to take note of.

They will include this component in their article and be asked to analyze the similarities and differences of said component between the two countries. By doing so we provide the student with a basic structure for his/her final article, stimulate them to learn and formulate personally informed opinions and decisions, and provide them with common subjects while encouraging a constructive debate in a controlled, dedicated environment.

As preparation for the trip, interested students and parents will attend an information session at the Embassy of Bahrain. Participants will have the opportunity to meet and speak with the Bahraini Ambassador to the United States and members of ETHNICK’s team. ETHNICK’s staff will provide students with an information package regarding the details of the trip.

The group of students and chaperones will travel to Manama from June 15 to June 25, 2019. Accompanied by two chaperones and met by two ETHNICK board members upon arrival in Bahrain, the ten to twenty students will spend the week learning about the history, culture and government of Bahrain guided by the staff of our partner host organization, the Ministry of Youth and Sports (MOYS). Students will have the opportunity to visit local museums and attractions as well as some of the main institutions of the Bahraini state.

Upon completion of the program students will have learned more about the ever-evolving world they live in while also developing a network of like-minded individuals ready to tackle whatever problems they are confronted with in a more determined and confident manner.
